What sponsoring codeweb pays for

Everything that runs on your machine is free forever. No accounts, no telemetry, no license keys. codeweb's claims are tested by running real AI agents against it, and those test runs cost money — sponsorship pays for them, and for adding support for new languages.

Where the money goes

Every benchmark run means paying AI providers for the agents it uses, and today that comes out of one person's pocket. Adding a language means building and checking its parser. Sponsorship pays for both. Nothing is paywalled.

Tiers

  • $5/mo — helps cover the recurring test and benchmark bills.
  • $25/mo — vote on the next language (C/C++ is the most requested) and get new-language prereleases up to 30 days early. Fixes and security updates always ship to everyone at once.
  • $250/mo (org) — your name on the site and README, priority issue triage.

Enterprise support

Email support with an SLA, onboarding help, and priority on feature requests. $3–6k/yr, limited to a few customers. Contact via the GitHub profile with "codeweb support" in the subject.

No local feature will ever move behind a payment. Sponsor prompts appear at most once a month, only after codeweb has caught real regressions for you, and never in agent responses or error messages.
