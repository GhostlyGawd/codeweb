What sponsoring codeweb pays for

Everything that runs on your machine is free forever — the engine, all {{toolCount}} MCP tools, the hooks, the report, the CLI, the CI gate, every language, every fix. Sponsoring pays for the two things that actually cost money: API tokens for the agent benchmarks behind the published claims, and per-language grammar verification.

Where the money goes

codeweb publishes measured claims, and the measurements aren't free: the agent benchmark runs are paid API tokens, and each new language needs its wasm grammar built and verified from source. Sponsorship funds that work — nothing is ever paywalled.

Tiers

  • $5/mo — keeps CI and the benchmark corpus running.
  • $25/mo — vote on the next language (C/C++ is the most requested) and get new-language prereleases up to 30 days early. Fixes and security updates always ship to everyone at once.
  • $250/mo (org) — your name on the site and README, priority issue triage.

Enterprise support

For orgs running codeweb across many repos: SLA'd email support, onboarding help, architecture-rules setup (codeweb.rules.json), and priority on feature work. $3–6k/yr flat, limited to a few customers. Contact via the GitHub profile with "codeweb support" in the subject.

The promises: no local feature will ever move behind a payment. No accounts, no telemetry, no license keys. Sponsor prompts appear at most once a month, only after codeweb has caught real regressions for you, and never in agent responses or error messages.
